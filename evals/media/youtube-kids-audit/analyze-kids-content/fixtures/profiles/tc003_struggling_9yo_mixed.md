## Cognitive style

**Learning type:** Visual/Spatial — learns through diagrams, spatial reasoning,
construction, and seeing how things fit together. Struggles significantly with
text-heavy learning.

**Key traits:**
> Jordan is a strong visual-spatial thinker who builds complex Lego sets by reading
> the visual instructions (not the text). Spatial reasoning is a clear strength.
> Reading and text-processing are significant challenges — 2 years behind grade level.
> Jordan is a reluctant reader and avoids text whenever possible. Math struggles are
> closely tied to reading difficulty (word problems are nearly impossible).
> Engagement spikes with visual content, spatial tasks, and anything related to
> Lego or Minecraft. Strong at pattern recognition when content is visual.

## Parent notes

> Jordan is 9 but working at grade-2 level in both math and reading.
> Do NOT recommend Beast Academy or grade-5 math — completely inappropriate.
> Numberblocks visual pattern content IS appropriate given the grade-2 math level —
> do not flag it as junk food just because Jordan is 9.
> The reading gap is severe but Jordan engages strongly with audiobooks and graphic
> novels. Recommend audiobooks (especially nonfiction with visuals) rather than
> demanding independent text reading.
> The Lego and Minecraft interests are Trojan Horse opportunities: use spatial
> building to teach multiplication area models, coordinate geometry, and fractions.
> Passive gaming consumption (watching others play livestreams) is a major concern —
> 8+ videos of passive Minecraft watching with no educational value.
> Priority: build confidence through areas of strength (spatial, visual) before
> pushing on text-heavy weaknesses.
